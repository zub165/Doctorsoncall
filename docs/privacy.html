Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2026

Docs On Call (“we”, “our”, “the app”) provides telehealth and medical record tools. This policy describes how we handle information when you use our mobile app and services at api.docsoncalls.com.

Information we collect

  • Account data — name, email, role (patient, provider, administrator), and authentication tokens.
  • Health & clinical data — appointments, triage vitals, medical records, documents, and notes you enter or upload.
  • Location — when you allow it, to show nearby hospitals and map features.
  • Device data — device type, OS version, and crash/diagnostic logs for support.
  • Purchases — subscription status via Apple and our billing provider where applicable.

How we use information

  • Provide booking, records, triage, and provider workflows
  • Authenticate users and enforce role-based access
  • Improve reliability, security, and support
  • Comply with legal obligations

Sharing

We do not sell your personal health information. Data may be shared with healthcare providers you choose, service providers that host our API, and authorities when required by law.

Storage & security

Data is transmitted over HTTPS and stored on secured servers. Offline copies on your device use local storage where supported. No method is 100% secure; use a strong password and protect your device.

Your choices & account deletion

  • Revoke location permission in iOS Settings
  • Delete your account in the app: Settings → Delete account (permanent; cannot be undone)
  • Or email us: zub165@yahoo.com — see Delete account & data for details
  • Manage Apple subscriptions in Settings → Apple ID → Subscriptions

Children

The app is not directed to children under 13 without parental consent as required by applicable law.

Changes

We may update this policy; continued use after changes means you accept the revised policy.
